02 — What's worth resolving

Tensions identified

Five specific observations from the captured surface — the load-bearing input for the redesign.

dated-pattern

Fragments reduced to wallpaper

A centered headline + one small orange button float over a motion-blurred particle field — the 2019 B2B-SaaS template. The most ownable asset works as background texture, not meaning.

ia-clutter

Flat, undifferentiated product grid

Six product cards (Enrichment Engine, Aggregation, Data Explorer, Insights, Embedded, Payments) carry identical weight. A buyer can't tell the platform's spine or where to start.

contrast-density

Low contrast, under-designed mid-page

71% of images have empty alt text; body is light grey #666 at 14–18px. The product-grid → case-study stretch is so uniformly white it reads under-designed rather than premium.

cliché

Two auto-advancing carousels

Trust and proof ride the two most default B2B devices — a rotating client-logo carousel and a rotating case-study slider. Auto-rotation hides content; six strong named cases (Nationwide, Mercer, Admiral, Loqbox…) are buried.

missed-opportunity

The one asset competitors can't copy is the one most wasted

The data-fragments motif is the value proposition made visible — "collect, enrich, contextualise the data fragments." It sits as a static, blurred decoration in the hero and never reappears: it never moves, never structures a section, never carries the brand below the fold.

03 — Three directions

The variants

All three are brand-faithful: the captured white/slate/orange palette, Raleway + Lato, the real Moneyhub wordmark and the real client logos. They differ by axis — A fixes the shape, B bets on colour, C bets on motion.

ARisk-averse green-light

Tomorrow's version of the site you have today

"Yes, that's us — fixed."

The captured IA kept intact, with the five tensions resolved.

  • De-blur the fragments; let the hero breathe; one confident orange CTA
  • Give the product grid a hierarchy — a lead "spine" product, the rest supporting
  • Replace the auto-advancing case slider with a scannable named 3-up
  • Real alt text everywhere; darken body copy for readable contrast
  • One radius discipline; a real --primary home for the orange

CVisionary pitch

What if motion was part of the identity?

"Kinetic data — a living pipeline."

Cinematic register: live-systems · auto-picked because Moneyhub is data-led infrastructure — the brand's core metaphor is data in motion.

Captured trait amplified: the data-fragments swarm stops being wallpaper and becomes a living financial-data pipeline.

05 — Notes

What's real vs. placeholder

The Moneyhub wordmark, the real client logos (Nationwide, Lloyds, Standard Life, Mercer, Admiral, Paragon, Scottish Widows…) and the named case-study brands are captured from the live site. Specific impact figures inside case studies are rendered as [data-placeholder] and should be confirmed before anything ships.

Validation: all three render with zero console errors; Mode A pinned. Variant C additionally passes motion validation — every animation neutralizes to the static reference under reduced-motion, and no headings animate.
